### Progression & Recovery Guidelines (Arnold Week)

**Progression:**
- Add 2.5-5 lbs when hitting top of rep range for all working sets
- Prioritize form and mind-muscle connection over ego lifting
- Track workouts weekly — aim for progressive overload in either weight or reps

**Deload:**
- Every 6-8 weeks, reduce volume by 50% for one week
- Keep intensity moderate (RPE 6-7) to allow recovery

**Recovery:**
- Sleep 7-9 hours nightly
- Consume 1g protein per lb of bodyweight
- Stay hydrated — minimum 1 gallon water daily
- Take rest day seriously — growth happens during recovery

### Progression & Recovery Guidelines (Platz Week)

**Progression:**
- On high-rep squat days, aim to add 1-2 reps weekly at same weight
- On heavy compounds, add 5 lbs when hitting top of rep range
- Track RPE — leg days should be RPE 9-10 on working sets

**Deload:**
- Every 4-6 weeks, reduce leg volume by 50%
- Keep one heavy day, one light day for active recovery

**Recovery:**
- Leg days are brutal — prioritize sleep (8-9 hours)
- Post-workout nutrition within 30 minutes
- Consider glutamine and BCAAs during training
- Take rest days seriously — legs need 48-72 hours to recover

### Progression & Recovery Guidelines (Nippard Week)

**Progression:**
- Track RIR (Reps in Reserve) on every set — target RIR 1-3 for hypertrophy
- Add weight when you can complete all sets at RIR 1 or less
- Use a training log — track weight, reps, and RIR weekly

**Deload:**
- Every 6-8 weeks, reduce volume by 40-50%
- Keep RIR at 3-4 for all working sets during deload week

**Recovery:**
- Sleep 7-9 hours — critical for recovery
- Nutrition: 1.6-2.2g protein per kg bodyweight
- Hydration: Minimum 35ml per kg bodyweight
- Consider creatine monohydrate (5g daily)
- Use RIR as autoregulation tool — adjust based on recovery status
